## Lab Steps

### Step 1: Analyze Content Website and Goals

**What to Do:**
Analyze the content website and identify goals:

1. **Review content website:**
   - Access your WordPress blog or provided content website example
   - Review website structure and content library
   - Identify website type (blog, publisher, content site)
   - Note content characteristics and topics

2. **Identify content site goals:**
   - Traffic growth (primary goal for content sites)
   - Topical authority building
   - Audience building
   - Engagement and retention
   - Other content objectives

3. **Document analysis:**
   - Record website type and characteristics
   - List content site goals identified
   - Note primary vs secondary goals
   - Document goal analysis

**Tool to Use:**
WordPress Blog or Provided Content Website Examples, Documentation Tool

**What to Record:**
Record goal analysis in the Submission Template, Section 1:
- Website type and characteristics
- Content site goals identified
- Primary vs secondary goals
- Goal analysis summary

**Checkpoint:**
You've completed this step when:
- [ ] You have analyzed the content website
- [ ] You have identified content site goals
- [ ] You have recorded goal analysis in the Submission Template

**What to Do:**
Design content strategy for growth:

1. **Plan content topics:**

   - Design content calendar approach
   - Plan content types (blog posts, guides, resources)
   - Plan content frequency and volume
   - Design scalable content creation process

3. **Plan keyword targeting:**
   - Identify target keywords per topic
   - Plan keyword coverage across topics
   - Design keyword targeting strategy
   - Plan keyword expansion approach

4. **Document content strategy:**
   - Record content topics planned

   - Create content strategy summary

### Step 5: Create Scalable Growth Plan

**What to Do:**
Create scalable content SEO growth plan:

1. **Plan growth phases:**
   - Phase 1: Foundation (initial content, basic linking)
   - Phase 2: Expansion (content growth, linking development)
   - Phase 3: Authority (topical authority, comprehensive coverage)
   - Plan phase timelines and milestones

2. **Plan scalability:**
   - Design scalable content creation process
   - Plan scalable internal linking approach
   - Design scalable keyword targeting
   - Plan scalable growth metrics

3. **Plan KPIs:**
   - Traffic growth KPIs (organic traffic, traffic by topic)
   - Authority KPIs (topical authority signals, rankings)
   - Engagement KPIs (time on site, pages per session)
   - Growth KPIs (content growth, linking growth)

4. **Document growth plan:**
   - Record growth phases planned
   - Document scalability approach
   - Record KPI framework
   - Create growth plan summary

## Troubleshooting

### Issue: I don't have enough content on my WordPress blog

**Symptoms:**
Your WordPress blog doesn't have enough content for planning.

**Solution:**
1. Use provided content website examples for planning practice
2. Plan for your blog as if it will grow (future planning)
3. Focus on understanding the growth planning framework
4. Document that you used examples or future planning

**If This Doesn't Work:**
The lab can be completed using provided examples or future planning. The learning is in the planning framework, not perfect content analysis.

---

### Issue: I don't understand topical authority

**Symptoms:**
You're uncertain about topical authority planning.

**Solution:**
1. Review Day 17 Chapter 1 for topical authority guidance
2. Topical authority: Comprehensive coverage of topic areas
3. Topic clusters: Related topics grouped together
4. Authority signals: Quality, depth, comprehensive coverage
5. When in doubt, document your understanding

**If This Doesn't Work:**
Focus on understanding the topical authority framework conceptually. The learning is in the planning process, not perfect authority planning.

---

### Issue: I'm not sure how to design internal linking

**Symptoms:**
You're uncertain about internal linking strategy.

**Solution:**
1. Review Day 17 Chapter 1 for internal linking guidance
2. Link structure: Site hierarchy and link flow
3. Linking patterns: Contextual, navigation, topic cluster linking
4. Link optimization: Anchor text, relevance, distribution
5. When in doubt, document your reasoning

**If This Doesn't Work:**
Focus on understanding the internal linking framework conceptually. The learning is in the planning process, not perfect linking design.
