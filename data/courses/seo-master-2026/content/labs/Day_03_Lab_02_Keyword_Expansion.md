### Primary Tools

**Google Search (Autocomplete, People Also Ask, Related Searches)**

- **Purpose:** Ahrefs provides additional keyword data, search volume estimates, and expansion opportunities when available. Limited access still enables valuable keyword discovery.
- **Access:** Limited access provided during training (if available)

## Lab Steps

### Step 1: Select Seed Keywords for Expansion

**What to Do:**
Select 3-4 seed keywords from your Day 3, Lab 1 results (or select new seed keywords if you haven't completed Lab 1):
1. Review your seed keywords from Lab 1
2. Select 3-4 seed keywords that represent different aspects of your niche
3. Ensure selected keywords are broad enough to expand but specific enough to be meaningful

**Tool to Use:**
No tool needed—use your seed keywords from Lab 1 or select new ones.

**What to Record:**
Record your selected seed keywords in the Submission Template, Section 1:
- List of 3-4 seed keywords you will expand
- Brief note on why you selected these keywords

**Checkpoint:**
You've completed this step when:
- [ ] You have selected 3-4 seed keywords for expansion
- [ ] You have documented your selections in the Submission Template

---

### Step 2: Expand Using Google Autocomplete

**What to Do:**
For each seed keyword, use Google Autocomplete to discover related keywords:
1. Go to Google Search
2. Type your seed keyword
3. Observe all autocomplete suggestions
4. Add modifiers (location, "best", "how to", etc.) and observe suggestions
5. Document all relevant keyword variations discovered

**Tool to Use:**
Google Search (Autocomplete)

**What to Record:**
Record your findings in the Submission Template, Section 2:
- For each seed keyword, list keywords discovered through autocomplete
- Note patterns in autocomplete suggestions

**Checkpoint:**
You've completed this step when:
- [ ] You have expanded all seed keywords using autocomplete
- [ ] You have documented discovered keywords in the Submission Template

---

### Step 3: Expand Using People Also Ask

**What to Do:**
For each seed keyword, use People Also Ask to discover question-based keywords:
1. Search for your seed keyword on Google
2. Locate the People Also Ask box
3. Expand questions to see answers and related questions
4. Document all question-based keywords discovered
5. Repeat for multiple questions in the PAA box

**Tool to Use:**
Google Search (People Also Ask)

**What to Record:**
Record your findings in the Submission Template, Section 3:
- For each seed keyword, list question-based keywords from People Also Ask
- Note the types of questions that appear

**Checkpoint:**
You've completed this step when:
- [ ] You have expanded all seed keywords using People Also Ask
- [ ] You have documented question-based keywords in the Submission Template

---

### Step 4: Expand Using Related Searches

**What to Do:**
For each seed keyword, use Related Searches to discover variations:
1. Search for your seed keyword on Google
2. Scroll to the bottom of the SERP

4. Click on related searches to discover more variations
5. Document all keyword variations discovered

**Tool to Use:**
Google Search (Related Searches)

**What to Record:**
Record your findings in the Submission Template, Section 4:
- For each seed keyword, list keywords discovered through related searches
- Note patterns in related search suggestions

**Checkpoint:**
You've completed this step when:

## Troubleshooting

### Issue: Can't find enough keywords

**Solution:**
- Try different modifiers with your seed keywords
- Expand People Also Ask questions more thoroughly
- Click through multiple related searches
- Consider that some niches have limited keyword variations

### Issue: People Also Ask doesn't appear

**Solution:**
- Try different seed keywords
- Some queries don't have People Also Ask boxes
- Use other expansion methods as primary sources
- Focus on autocomplete and related searches

### Issue: Ahrefs not available

**Solution:**
- Complete the lab using only free tools
- Document in your submission that you used free tools only
- Focus on thorough expansion using autocomplete, PAA, and related searches
- Note that free tools can be highly effective when used systematically
